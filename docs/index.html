  • The test environment is comprised of a room with large green spheres that act as targets. These targets turn on one at a time at random. When the gaze intersects with the target, the light turns off, the reaction-time is recorded, and the next target is queued. The smaller green, red, and blue spheres mark the locations of the left pupil, center gaze, and right pupil, respectively.
  • The flight simulator was developed by two of our team members and other students prior to the start of this project. Our team integrated our eye-tracking and used ray casts to determine gaze locations.
  • On the intersection of the gaze raycast and an object collider, the coordinates are recorded. The coordinate data is normalized, colored based on concentration, and generated as cubes within the simulation environment.
